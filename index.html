Paper PDF

Motion blur can hinder the ability to analyze and draw significant medical conclusions from MRI images. As motion blur cannot always be prevented, techniques to remove motion artifacts are crucial to patient well being. By adapting U-Net and adding a layer to blind-deconvolve blur, deep learning can be explored as a technique to de-blur MRI images. With computationally-limited training, the architecture trained well and showed potential for use as a method of MRI de-blurring. Quantitative and qualitative analysis show that using a custom loss function could provide for much stronger de-blurred image prediction.
